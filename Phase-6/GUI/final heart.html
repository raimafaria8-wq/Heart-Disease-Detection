Learning Curve — Data Ablation
Performance increases sub-linearly with data. The 50%→100% jump yields only +1.3pp AUC, suggesting the model may be approaching saturation and additional augmentation (VAE) is more impactful than raw data volume.
Ablation Impact Summary
⚠️ Key Finding
Transfer learning contributes the single largest gain (+5.3pp AUC). Removing TL degrades performance more than any other ablation, confirming pre-training is essential on this small dataset.

Highest prediction entropy cases reviewed manually. Distribution:
Genuinely ambiguous (near-boundary features) 9 / 20
Suspected label noise 5 / 20
Atypical feature combinations 4 / 20
OOD patients (extreme age/BMI) 2 / 20
📌 Finding
5/20 hard examples showed conflicting labels (e.g., HD diagnosis absent despite high IVSD + T-wave inversion). Suggests ~2–4% label noise in Erbil ECG dataset — a meaningful limitation.
Honest Limitations (Phase 5 — Required Section)
Technical Limitations
📌 Dataset size: 634 patients is small by DL standards. Confidence intervals are wide. Generalisation to other populations unproven.
📌 Label noise: ~2–4% estimated label noise detected via hard-example mining. True labels not re-adjudicated.
📌 Tabular-only: The clinical model does not process raw ECG waveforms or imaging directly — only structured feature extractions.
📌 Calibration: Brier=0.073 is good but model overestimates risk in the 60–80% probability range (calibration plot shows upward bias).
Clinical / Fairness Limitations
📌 Geographic bias: Trained on Erbil (Kurdish-Iraqi) population. Direct deployment in other demographics requires validation and possible re-calibration.
📌 Sex imbalance: Dataset 68% male. Female subgroup F1 is 3.2pp lower (0.904 vs 0.937). Model may under-perform in women, especially for atypical presentations.
📌 No temporal data: Model sees a single clinical snapshot. Longitudinal trajectory (worsening vs stable) is not captured.
📌 Not validated in acute settings: Training patients were stable outpatients. ED/ICU presentations may differ substantially.
